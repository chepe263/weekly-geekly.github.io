J & P: You can make good money on smartphones

J'son & Partners predicts that by the end of 2006 over 1.21 million smartphones will be sold in Russia. The first half of the year in the market for these devices corresponded to 500,000 smartphones sold with a sales volume of $ 200 million. Over the year, the market grew by more than 100%. Now, as the authors of the study note, Russian buyers are ready to spend from $ 400 to $ 600 on the purchase of a smartphone.

The most popular with the owners of these devices are Internet services, instant messaging services, E-mail (including Push E-mail), RSS news. In addition, smartphones are valued for the ability to read books from them and listen to music. In the future, LBS services (Location Based Services, are implemented based on subscriber location), access services to the corporate network, security services, as well as advertising and mobile TV will enjoy success.

Now in 50% of cases, a smartphone is bought for reasons of prestige. In 2007, when together with the expansion of communication services and content services will be sold, it is estimated that about 2 million devices, the smartphone will become a more familiar thing. According to forecasts, by the end of 2007, smartphones will account for 5% of the total mobile phone market in Russia.
