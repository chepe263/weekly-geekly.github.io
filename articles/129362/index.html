Mockup.io is a service for demonstrating layouts of iOS interfaces.



Colleagues, over the past 2.5 years I have worked with my team on more than 40 iOS applications and over 100 versions of them. During this time, we saw thousands of blanks, layouts and final designs. With each project, we are increasingly assimilating the importance of a piece of design in the design process. In this connection, we ventured to create our own product, which I want to talk about today.

Mockup.io - a service for demonstrating interface layouts for iOS


With it, you can really conveniently show mockups in a browser or directly on the iPhone and iPad
  • View modes automatically adjust to the client device
  • In the web version for the desktop there are 3 view modes: Grid, Realistic, Fullsize
  • Quick navigation using the keyboard (hot keys)
  • Rename, rearrange, add and delete layouts on the fly (ajax, drag & drop)
  • To make it more realistic on an iOS device, you can add a project like a WebApp on a springboard
  • To demonstrate the client, just send a short link, for example, mockup.io/9859

First of all, we sought to solve our internal pressing problems. Already we are using Mockup.io - we show mockups both within the team, as well as customers and friends to receive feedback.

In the plans:

  • iOS app on the App Store
  • QR codes for quick viewing from mobile devices
  • Drag & drop new versions of layouts over old ones
  • Versioned layouts
  • Comments
  • API
The current version is simple, but already useful. To get started, you need a Google Account.
