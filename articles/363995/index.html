Albert Einstein digital archive has opened

The Digital Einstein project , supervised by Professor Diana Kormos-Buchwald of the California Institute of Technology, opened on Friday. The archive currently contains 13 volumes of thirty planned for publication, each of which contains about 5,000 documents, starting from the earliest moments of the life of an outstanding physicist.

The source material for the project was the personal archive of Albert Einstein, which he bequeathed to Princeton and the Hebrew University in Jerusalem and some of which were previously available on the Internet. For many documents, an English translation has been made with convenient switching, so there should be no problems with German texts.

With this document, you can personally evaluate the famous legend of the great scientist's school assessments and see for yourself his assessment in physics.
