Secret IP addresses of German intelligence

A secret document has been published on the Wikileaks website, stolen from the postal correspondence of the country's largest Internet provider T-Systems (Deutsche Telekom). This document lists more than two dozen IP addresses that are used by the German intelligence services BND (Bundesnachrichtendienst). A Whois search indicates that addresses are registered to the same dummy company.

Preliminary analysis confirms the authenticity of the document. In addition, evidence of this authenticity is the fact that the provider has already sent a letter to Wikileaks requesting to immediately delete this PDF file.

This leak can be very painful for German spies. After all, now it is possible to analyze logs of web servers and study in detail which sites, for example, in Russia, they visited. Even the analysis of randomly indexed logs in Yandex already gives some information.
